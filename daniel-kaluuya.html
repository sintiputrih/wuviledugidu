Likewise, this personality attended Torriano Primary School. Later on, he attended St Aloysius’ College, Highgate. Subsequently, this personality took A-level History, Drama, and Biology at Camden School for Girls mixed semi-comprehensive sixth-form college.

Talking about his family, Kaluuya has an elder sister whose name is still a mystery. Likewise, his parents are Damalie Namusoke and Stephen Kaluuya.

Similarly, he grew up with his mother and sister on a council estate in Camden Town. On the other hand, his father lived in Balaka, Malawi. He didn’t have any contact with his father until the age of 15 years. Furthermore, Daniel is British by nationality and his ethnicity is Ugandan.

Career

Professionally, Daniel Kaluuya is a famous actor. As such, he is prominent both on screen and on stage and has received numerous accolades. Some of them are an Academy Award, two BAFTA Awards, two Screen Actors Guild Awards, and a Golden Globe Award. Similarly, he also received nominations for a Primetime Emmy Award and a Laurence Olivier Award.

Fairly recently in 2021, Time magazine named Daniel among the 100 most influential people in the world. Talking about the beginning, this actor started his acting career as a teenager in improvisational theatre. Subsequently, this actor played the role of Posh Kenneth in the 1st two seasons of Skins. For this series, this actor also co-wrote some of the episodes.

Similarly, for his lead role in Sucker Punch at the Royal Court Theatre, this actor won both the Evening Standard Award and Critics’ Circle Theatre Award for “Outstanding Newcomer”. Also, this actor received the BAFTA Rising Star Award in 2018.

Likewise, in the late 2000s and early 2010s, this actor gained further notice for his performances as Michael “Tealeaf” Fry in Psychoville, Michael “Mac” Armstrong in The Fades, Barclay in the Doctor Who Easter special, and Bingham “Bing” Madsen in the Black Mirror episode “Fifteen Million Merits”.

More Details

Moreover, this personality also appeared as Agent Colin Tucker in the 2011 film Johnny English Reborn and as Black Death in the 2013 film Kick-Ass 2. Also, this actor had a supporting role in Sicario in 2015.

Additionally, this actor achieved a career breakthrough after he starred in Get Out in 2017. This gained actor Daniel a nomination for the Academy Award for “Best Actor”. He followed this with roles in Black Panther, Widows, Queen & Slim, and Peele’s Nope.

For his role as the Black Panther Party leader Fred Hampton in Judas and the Black Messiah in 2021, this actor won the Golden Globe Award, Critics’ Choice Award,  Academy Award, BAFTA Award, and Screen Actors Guild Award for the category “Best Supporting Actor”.

Similarly, this actor became the 7th youngest winner of the Academy Award for “Best Supporting Actor” at age 32 years. Likewise, this personality is also the 1st British actor of African heritage to win an Academy Award.
