Table of Biography

Irrfan Khan was an Indian actor. Irrfan is professionally also popular as only “Irrfan”. He was a part of mostly Hindi cinema and was also a part of several British as well as American films. Sadly, the veteran actor died at the young age of 53 years on Wednesday, 29th April 2020, just four days after his mother’s passing away.

Early Life and Childhood

Irrfan Khan’s full name is Sahabzade Irfan Ali Khan and he was born on 7 January 1967. His birthplace is Tonk, Rajasthan, India and he was 53 years old when passed away on 29 April 2020 in Mumbai, Maharashtra, India. He was born to a Muslim family of Pathan ancestry. His mother is Saeeda Begum Khan and she was from Jodhpur, and his father is Yaseen Ali Khan who was from the Khajuriya village in Rajasthan’s Tonk district.

His parents ran a tire business and he spent his childhood in Tonk, and then Jaipur. He was good at cricket and hence he was selected to play in the CK Nayudu Trophy for emerging players in the under-23 category. It is a tournament seen as a stepping stone to first-class cricket in India. However, he did not attend because he could not afford travel expenses. Moreover, he has three siblings namely Salman Khan, Imran Khan, and Rukhsana Begum.

Tragically, only four days before his own decease, his 95-year-old mother also died in Jaipur. However, he and his family could not attend her funeral because of India’s nationwide lockdown during the global COVID-19 coronavirus pandemic.

Battle with Cancer and Untimely Demise

Bollywood star Irrfan Khan lost his life in his battle with a cancerous tumor on Wednesday, 29th April 2020. He passed away at Mumbai’s Kokilaben Dhirubhai Ambani Hospital. He had been getting treatment in London and returned to his home country, India, a few months back. He was rushed to Mumbai’s hospital a day before following colon infection and then admitted to the ICU. He had been battling the neuroendocrine tumor ever since its diagnosis in 2018.

Caption: Irrfan Khan’s funeral was arranged in the presence of his near ones amid India’s nationwide lockdown due to the global coronavirus pandemic. Source: Free Press Journal

The versatile actor was buried in the presence of his family, close relatives, and friends at 3 pm on Wednesday. An official statement from the actor’s representative read:

“Irrfan was buried at the Versova kabrastan in Mumbai at 3 pm this afternoon soon after the news of his passing away was announced. In presence were his family, close relatives and friends. Everyone paid their final respects and mourned the loss of his passing away. We pray for his peace and we hope he’s in a better place today. He was strong in his fight, and we all have to be strong too in this loss.”

Beginning of Acting

Then he showed interest in acting under the influence of his maternal uncle who was a theatre artist in Jodhpur. In Jaipur, he got introduced to noted theatre artists and he also did several stage performances in the city. Moreover, he completed his MA in Jaipur and later joined the National School of Drama (NSD) in New Delhi. He joined it in 1984 and studied acting.

Additionally, in his early days in Mumbai, he took up a job as an air conditioner repairman and visited the home of his acting inspiration, Rajesh Khanna, in 1984. Then later in an interview, he said:

“The kind of craze that Rajesh Khanna witnessed has not been duplicated by anyone. He was the biggest and the realest star Bollywood has produced. I’d say stardom is that feeling of being possessed by your idol; you are so overwhelmed with euphoria you lose touch with reality.”

Then in 2012, he changed his name’s spelling from “Irfan” to “Irrfan” stating that he liked the sound of the extra ‘r’ in his name. But there was no numerology behind this change. Further, according to a 2016 interview, the actor also later dropped “Khan” from his name because he wanted his work to define him, not his lineage.

Career and Professional Life

Irrfan Khan is/was an Indian actor who worked in Hindi cinema and also in British and American films. He is cited in the media as one of the finest actors in Indian cinema. His career spanned over 30 years and earned him numerous accolades which include a National Film Award, an Asian Film Award, and four Filmfare Awards. Similarly, the late actor also received the Padma Shri, which is India’s fourth-highest civilian honor.

Moreover, he is the first Bollywood actor to star in two films that won the Academy Award for Best Director. They are Slumdog Millionaire in 2008 and Life of Pi in 2012. Both these films also won the Academy Awards for “Best Cinematography” and “Best Original Score”. Additionally, in two completely unconnected movies, he spoke of “Richard Parker”: the tiger in Life of Pi (2012) and Spiderman’s dad in The Amazing Spider-Man (2012).

Similarly, his impressive filmography also includes projects like Maqbool, Haasil, Paan Singh Tomar, Haider, Pinku, Talvar, and Salaam Bombay!. Salaam Bombay! is an Academy Award-nominated movie. Also, he was also a part of international projects like The Namesake, Life Of Pi, Inferno, Slumdog Millionaire, Puzzle, and The Warrior among others.

The late actor’s last project was Angrezi Medium which included co-stars Kareena Kapoor and Radhika Madan. Similarly, his initial project/film was Haasil. He used to appear as a villain in several movies before getting the role of the hero in numerous movies.

Another one of his international projects is A Mighty Heart, a 2007 drama. Michael Winterbottom directed the movie based on Mariane Pearl’s 2003 memoir of the same name. It revolves around the aftermath of the kidnapping of Marianne’s journalist husband Daniel Pearl in Pakistan, and his subsequent beheading by the kidnappers. In the movie, Angelina Jolie played Marianne Pearl while he played the character of Karachi police chief Zeeshan Kazmi.

In addition to being an actor, he was also a brand ambassador for Resurgent Rajasthan. It is a campaign by the state government of Rajasthan and he got the appointment in September 2015. Moreover, he also lent his voice to the light and sound show at the war memorial for the Indian army. It was organized at Vidhan Sabha Road, Jaipur. The show portrayed the valor and courage of Rajasthani soldiers as well as their historic and modern triumphs in battle.

Awards and Nominations

Irrfan is a remarkable and legendary Bollywood actor who also starred in Bollywood movies. He received several awards and nominations. He has a total of ten nominations under his belt, among with he bagged seven awards. Some of them are “Best Performance in a Negative Role” at the 2004 Filmfare Awards for his role in Haasil, and “Best Supporting Actor” at the 2008 Filmfare Awards for his role in Life in a… Metro.

He also bagged “Best Actor” at the National Film Awards and Filmfare Awards in 2013 for his role in Paan Singh Tomar, “Best Actor” at the 2014 Asian Film Awards for The Lunchbox, as well as at the 2018 Filmfare Awards for his role in Hindi Medium.

Additionally, he also shared a photo on his Instagram in December 2017 in which he is receiving the DIFF honor for the movie The Song of Scorpions.

Irrfan Khan – Net Worth 2023

At the time of his death, his net worth estimation was $50 million. He earned it through his various acting projects in his home country as well as internationally. Many veteran actors in Bollywood described him as an irreplaceable actor in Indian Cinema. Some also called him the remarkable representative of Indian Cinema in the international arena.

Unlike most Indian stars, Irrfan was outspoken on religion. On Arnab Goswami’s talk show, Khan took on Muslim fundamentalists, including India’s Grand Imam. He argued against “transactional religious interaction” “personal religious discovery”, and “to discover yourself, to find God”.

Although he admits he is “not an authority” on the Quran and Islamic Holy scriptures, he bravely stood by his comments despite heavy criticism and threats of violence. Khan was very much aware of the dangers that his frank comments pose to him and his family. His wife also shared her family’s pride in the actor’s beliefs and comments.

Controversial Comment on Islamic Practices

Moreover, during an event in Jaipur, India in 2016, he stated that in today’s practices, the actual meaning of Qurbani (sacrifice) has been lost. He added that purchasing a goat from the market and killing it after two days is not “Qurbani”. The actor also added that the essence of the Islamic festival is about giving up and sacrificing something that is important to oneself.

The actor also condemned the ritual of fasting during the month of Ramzan. He said,

“Rather than fasting during Ramzan, people should self-introspect. Animals are being slaughtered on in the name of Qurbani during Muharram. We, Muslims, have made a mockery of Muharram. It is meant for mourning and what we do? Take out (tajiya) processions.”

He faced widespread criticism from Islamic clerics and even the AIMPLB for his remarks. Such people who hated the actor for his remarks on Islamic practices were quick to celebrate his demise. One of them is the so-called journalist, Ali Sohrab, infamous for sharing objectionable and bigoted content on his Twitter page. In one of his series of Tweets, Ali wrote that it is Allah’s ultimate punishment that the actor who had once insulted the ritual of fasting during the month of Ramzan, died during the month of Ramzan.

Apart from Sohrab, there are many other radical Islamists who have been mocking and celebrating the actor’s death. Many other Islamists also chose this day to scorn the departed soul for his progressive ideologies. A Twitter user Abu Salman wrote, “He was an atheist. He made fun of Ramadan. We can only pray for his family to get a holiday. Nothing more.”

Relationship Status

Irrfan Khan was a married man until his untimely death. He married his wife, Sutapa Sikdar, on 23 February 1995. The couple has two sons together and they are Babil and Ayan. Sutapa is a writer and fellow NSD graduate. She is also a dialogue writer in films. Similarly, his second son, Ayan, born in 2001 is also an actor who starred in Life of Pi.
