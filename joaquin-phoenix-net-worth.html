Early Life

Zachary Knight Galifianakis was born in Wilkesboro, North California on the 1st of October, 1969.

His mother has Scottish ancestry, while his grandparents were emigrants from Crete, Greece.

Galifianakis was baptized in his father’s Greek Orthodox church.

Galifianakis has a younger sister named Merritt, and an older brother named Greg.

He attended Wilkes Central High School and subsequently attended but did not graduate from North Carolina State University.

Zach began working at a public access station teaching waltz in 1991.

Career

Before breaking into the comedy circuit, Zach Galifianakis gained media experience working at a public access station in college.

He also spent time teaching waltz classes, where he encountered Mary J. Blige and forged connections in the entertainment industry.

His television debut on the show Boston Common would provide the opportunity to land a spot on Saturday Night Live and launch his career.

Early Career In Comedy Television Shows

While Zach Galifianakis only appeared on Saturday Night Live for two weeks, the experience was to be a pivotal moment in his early career.

His role led to a meeting with Will Ferrell, and by 2001, he landed a role in Comedy Central Presents, which came to the attention of VH1 producers.

Zach Galifianakis was then given his own talk show, Late World with Zach, in which he performed music and hosted many of his comedian friends.

Throughout this period of his career, Zach Galifianakis also made several guest appearances on the Jimmy Kimmel chat show, further raising his profile.

Additional work with Comedy Central soon followed, including the show Dog Bites Man and a guest-starring role on The Sarah Silverman Show.

This exposure to some of the richest comedians in the world allowed him to branch out from television with many notable film roles.

It also gave him a long list of contacts for potential guests for his most popular show to date, Between Two Ferns, on the Funny or Die website.

Celebrity Interviews For Between Two Ferns

Galifianakis took his career in exciting new directions with Between Two Ferns, the comedy talk show that featured many famous guests.

He hosted high-profile comedians, including Ben Stiller and Steve Carell, many of whom he would later collaborate with on movies.

His interview style, which included irreverent observations and surreal questions, quickly launched him into the comedy world spotlight.

As a result, the show also attracted the attention of A-list celebrities and movie stars who were eager to appear on the show.

Hollywood stars such as Natalie Portman and Sean Penn made guest appearances on Between Two Ferns, leading to his first Primetime Emmy Award.

The talk show format even included notable American politicians in its line-up, with Hillary Clinton a notable example of a powerful political guest.

Notable Movie Roles

Throughout his career, Zach Galifianakis has appeared in many successful comedy movies with some of the biggest names in entertainment.

His most successful movie in terms of box office revenue, The Hangover, saw him performing alongside Bradley Cooper and Ken Jeong in the film.

Before this breakthrough performance, he had made several appearances in smaller comedy movies and low-budget independent releases.

The success of The Hangover was followed by many popular comedies, with notable examples including Dinner for Schmucks and The Muppets.

Galifianakis also played the starring role in movies, including The Campaign, while continuing to provide supporting roles for his fellow comedians.

In addition to his acting roles, he also worked as a voice actor in The Lego Batman Movie and G-Force, for which he also worked on the video game.

Appearances In Music Videos

Zach Galifianakis has enjoyed a successful career outside of television and movies, most notably as a frequent actor in high-profile music videos.

He appeared in two notable music videos in 2007, including the video for Kanye West’s track “Can’t Tell Me Nothing” and “Not About Love” by Fiona Apple.
