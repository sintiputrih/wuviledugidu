David Rudisha: Early life, Family, and Education

David Rudisha was born and raised in Kilgoris, Kenya, by Daniel Rudisha and Naomi Rudisha.

Being born in a family of athletes, David was heavily interested in sports and started sprinting.

Furthermore, David attended Kimuron Secondary School and St. Patrick’s High School in Iten, Kenya.

Similarly, he trained with Japheth Kimutai, who later recommended him to James Templeton.

Likewise, Rudisha joined the group of runners managed by Templeton and became a 400-meter runner.

However, his coach, Irishman Colm O’Connell, suggested Rudisha to try 800 m.

David Rudisha: Career

David started his career early and made his debut in 2005 when he was 14 years of age.

David’s first Olympic experience came in 2012, after his guaranteed selection for the Kenyan Olympic team with a win at the Kenyan trials.

Also, the athlete managed to set the world record of 1:40.91 for the 800 m at the London 2012 Olympics.

Moreover, David was also given the Association of National Olympic Committees Award for Best Male Athlete.
