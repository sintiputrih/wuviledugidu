Who Is Gio Benitez?, Gio Benitez Biography and Wiki

Gio Benitez is an American broadcast journalist as well as a correspondent for ABC News, who appears on Good Morning America, World News Tonight, 20/20, and Nightline. He was born in the Miami United States as Giovani “Gio” Benitez.

He also hosts the Fusion collaboration version of Nightline. Benitez has won three television news Emmy Awards. Gio Benitez was promoted to Transportation Correspondent, operating from New York and DC on April 9, 2020.

He is known to be an award-winning reporter and has covered a wide range of stories for the network, including the Pulse nightclub shooting, El Chapo’s underground escape from a Mexican prison, and the Boston Marathon bombing.

Benitez frequently breaks exclusive investigative stories as part of Good Morning America’s series, “GMA Investigates.” Some of these investigations have led to massive consumer product safety recalls. He was a reporter for WFOR, the CBS owned-and-operated TV station in Miami, where he covered the 2012 Presidential election and reported extensively on the Trayvon Martin case before joining ABC News in 2013.

Benitez traveled to Haiti to cover relief efforts following the devastating earthquake in January 2010. His return flight to Miami was turned into a rescue mission, as he traveled with injured Haitian evacuees to the island of Curaçao. He was the first reporter to ever shoot a TV story entirely with an iPhone in June 2009.

Benitez received two national Emmy Awards, two regional Emmy Awards, and is an eight-time nominee. Most notably, Benitez was nominated for his investigative series on alleged police brutality which resulted in two Miami officers being forced to turn in their guns and badges.

Benitez was an investigative producer at WFOR and worked on stories involving Medicare fraud, public safety, and government corruption before becoming a reporter. He began at the station as an Emma L. Bowen Foundation work-study scholar.
