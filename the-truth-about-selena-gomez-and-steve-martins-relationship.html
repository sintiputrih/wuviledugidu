Fall is a great time of year to cozy up with a new TV show and Only Murders In The Building is both funny and thrilling. Selena plays Mabel, a young girl living in a beautiful NYC apartment building who teams up with Steve Martin's Charles and Martin Short's Oliver to create a true crime podcast about a neighbor who was killed.

Selena Gomez talked about her new show so her fans knew what to expect, and now that Only Murders In The Building is in full swing and fans are loving it, how well does she get along with her co-star Steve Martin? Let's take a look.

Do They Get Along?

Martin Short and Steve Martin's Netflix collaboration feels perfect, as the two comedians have been friends for a long time, and that makes people wonder how Steve Martin gets along with Selena Gomez.

Their characters become friends after looking into a murder in their apartment complex, but what do the stars think of each other in real life?

It sounds like Selena Gomez loves her co-stars and according to People, called Martin and Steve "humble and kind."

Selena also shared that the three actors e-mail each other regularly and that she has learned a lot from them: the star said, "I asked them about how life used to be, and it was always so interesting to hear about the industry and what it used to be like. I felt like a sponge, just absorbing everything I could. We're all on a group e-mail chain."

According to Todayonline.com, Selena Gomez shared that she had some nerves before making the TV show, but things worked out well and she calls Steve Martin and Martin Short her "uncles." The actress said, "I was so excited. And I was nervous because I didn't know what to expect of what I was getting myself into. Now I have these two crazy uncles in my life who give me boy advice and I sing rap songs to them."

Steve Martin talked about his working relationship with Selena Gomez and said that they got along well right away.

According to Entertainment Tonight, Steve Martin and Martin Short were interviewed on The Howard Stern Show on SiriusXM and Steve said, “I knew who Selena Gomez was, but I didn’t know her work that well. So, I looked her up on the internet and I realized at 28 she had done as many movies as I had. We hit it off really well, almost immediately. Then as the series went on we developed that kind of rapport on the set that you want so badly."

From watching the show, it definitely looks like Steve Martin and Selena Gomez's characters formed a beautiful and sweet friendship, as Charles and Mabel slowly learned more about each other as they attempted to solve the mystery. They had a light-hearted relationship, as in a hilarious scene, Charles wasn't sure if she should call or text Mabel and finally settled on a text message, but he typed a very formal message as that's just the kind of person that he is.

When all three actors were interviewed by MTV, Selena Gomez said, "I was very lucky" and that she and Steve Martin worked togehter on the first day of filming the show. She said "it was instant" and that he made her feel "comfortable." Selena also called the experience "magical" and shared that they really welcomed her onto set. Selena said Martin Short and Steve Martin made sure that she was totally included, which is really sweet to hear about.
