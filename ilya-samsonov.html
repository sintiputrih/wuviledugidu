Career

Samsonov, who grew up in Magnitogorsk, Russia, made his Kontinental Hockey League (KHL) debut in relief with Metallurg Magnitogorsk during the 2014-15 season. Samsonov was regarded as the best European goalie in the 2015 NHL Entry Draft after a good junior season with Metallurg’s junior team, Stalnye Lisy. Despite skipping the scouting combine in Buffalo and performing no interviews with the organization, he was picked 22nd overall by the Washington Capitals. Samsonov began his NHL career after signing a three-year, entry-level deal with the Washington Capitals after the 2017-18 KHL season. Samsonov attended training camp with the Capitals but was reassigned to their American Hockey League (AHL) club, the Hershey Bears, before to the start of the 2018-19 season. He made his NHL debut the following season, serving as the main backup to starter Braden Holtby. Following his first victory, Samsonov went on to win ten more games, becoming just the third NHL rookie goalkeeper to do so and the first since 1973-74.

Samsonov, a free agent from the Capitals, chose to sign a one-year, $1.8 million deal with the Toronto Maple Leafs on July 13, 2022. Samsonov was recruited with the expectation of becoming the backup to Matt Murray, but after Murray was injured two games into the 2022-23 season, Samsonov was promoted to the team’s starting goalkeeper for at least the following four weeks.
