Biography

Orson Scott Card was born in Richland, WA on August 24, 1951. Science fiction author who penned the Hugo and Nebula Award-winning works, Ender’s Game and Speaker for the Dead. His other notable books include Seventh Son, Red Prophet, and Alvin Journeyman. His character, Ender Wiggin, was portrayed by Asa Butterfield in the 2013 film version of Ender’s Game. He focused on writing poetry while attending Brigham Young University, where he also studied theater. He began his writing career as an editor for Brigham Young University Press.
