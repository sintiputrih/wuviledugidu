Anna Kendrick was only 12 when she received her big break as a supporting actress in the Broadway musical High Society. She won a Theater World Award and earned a Tony nomination for her performance. Anna debuted in 2003 with the independent movie Fritzi. Kendrick also gained popularity for her supporting role in the movie series The Twilight Saga, a hit at the box office. Her career has grown exponentially ever since, and she has starred in commercially and critically successful movies. The star has received numerous nominations for her quirky yet dramatic performances.

From musical comedies to drama and thrillers, Anna Kendrick has starred in movies that have a stellar cast. These movies have also grossed millions at the box office. Let’s take a look at some of her highest-grossing films.

The Accountant (2016)

An action-thriller, The Accountant is a story that centers around Ben Affleck’s character of Christian Wolff. According to The Guardian, Kendrick stars in the movie as a supporting character Dana Cummings, an in-house accountant working for Living Robotics. The movie was made on a $44 million budget, was well-received by audiences, and made $152.9 million worldwide.

Up In The Air (2009)

A comedy-drama adapted from the 2001 novel of the same name, Up In The Air, made $166.8 million in theaters, a tremendous profit for a movie with a $25 million budget. Starring actors such as George Clooney, Vera Farmiga, and Jason Bateman, the film was praised by critics for the plot about a corporate downsizer. Kendrick portrayed the role of Natalie Keener, an ambitious woman working for Clooney’s character. Anna also won an Oscar nomination in the Best Supporting Actress category for her role.

Pitch Perfect 3 (2017)

The third sequel of Pitch Perfect sees the acapella group Barden Bellas reunite for the final time for a USO tour. Kendrick reprises her role as Becca and a stunning cast that includes Rebel Wilson, Anna Camp, Brittany Snow, and Hailee Steinfeld. With a $45 Million budget, the movie grossed $185.7 million at the box office worldwide.

Into The Woods (2014)

A musical spectacle directed by Rob Marshall, Into The Woods, was adapted from Stephen Sondheim’s songs. It was well-received by audiences, gaining $212.5 million. The movie featured characters from various fairy tales crossing paths with each other. Kendrick played the role of Cinderella opposite Chris Pine’s Prince Charming, as noted by IMDB. The film also starred Meryl Streep, Emily Blunt, Johnny Depp, and James Corden.

Pitch Perfect 2 (2014)

Starring in the highly anticipated sequel to Pitch Perfect, Pitch Perfect 2 was a bigger hit than the original movie due to its stunning cast, surprising cameos, and heartwarming storyline. Kendrick portrayed the role of college senior Becca, who is a part of the acapella group, the Barden Bellas. The movie was produced on a $31 million budget earned $287 million at the box office.

Trolls (2016)

A computer-animated musical comedy, Trolls sees Kendrick lending her voice to the leading character of Princess Poppy. Another lead of the movie, Branch, who Justin Timberlake voices. As mentioned by BBC, the movie broke the UK Box office record, making a whopping $6.59 million during its opening weekend. The film had a comprehensive collection of $343 million.

Twilight (2008)

Twilight saw Anna Kendrick playing the supporting role of Jessica Stanley, one of the high school students. The film put her in the spotlight along with the lead, including Robert Pattinson, Kristen Stewart, and Taylor Lautner. With a merely $37 million budget, the movie broke records, earning $402 million around the world.

The Twilight Saga: New Moon (2009)

New Moon saw Kendrick reprise Bella’s talkative friend Jessica Stanley and was offered a longer screen time as she had multiple solo scenes with Stewart’s character. The movie was a bigger success than the first one and made $687.5 million.

The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn- Part 1 (2011)

One of the most highly anticipated movies of 2011, Breaking Dawn Part 1, was the first part of the finale of The Twilight Saga. Stanley gained traction when she attended Bella and Edward’s wedding ceremony and gave a speech at the reception. The movie had a big opening weekend and earned $689 million overall at the box office.

The Twilight Saga: Eclipse (2010)

The third movie in The Twilight Saga, Eclipse, surpassed all expectations as it ranks the second-highest-grossing Twilight movie ever, making $706 million worldwide. As the characters graduate from high school, she gives a heartfelt speech during the graduation ceremony. As a result, the movie broke the midnight box-office record, earning $68 million, as reported by Screen Rant.

Her other notable movies include Pitch Perfect, Mike And Dave Need Wedding Dates, and Table 19. She is known for her comic and awkward characters. The actress was last seen starring in the 2021 science-fiction thriller Stowaway.
