Kylie Bracknell’s Husband Clint Bracknell Is A Singer And Language Revivalist

Kylie Bracknell’s husband, Clint Bracknell, is a Noongar musician and singer. He is also a professor of indigenous languages.

Clint’s award-winning Ph.D. research was based on the Noongar song. In May 2022, he released an album that consisted of Noongar language dance tracks. Clint worked with the ARIA Award-winning musician Paul Mac on the album. His music credits also include theater composition and sound design.

His music has been featured in international television shows, commercials, and feature films. He has won the Performing Arts WA Awards for Best Composition and has been nominated for Best Original Score at the Helpmann Awards.

Apart from his musical career, Bracknell is also a professor of indigenous languages at the University of Queensland. He is the deputy chair of the AIATSIS council and also a member of the advisory board at the ARC Centre of Excellence for the Dynamics of Language.

He was the recipient of the 2020 John Barrett Award for Australian Studies, the University of Sydney’s 2017 Wingara Mura Excellence Award, ECU’s 2021 Research Engagement Award, and UWA’s 2016 Robert Street Prize.

In 2016, Bracknell contributed his work to the United Nations International Expert Group Meeting on Indigenous Languages. He co-developed a major in contemporary music for the Sydney Conservatorium of Music.
