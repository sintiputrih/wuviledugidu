Volodymyr Zelenskyy Biography

Volodymyr Oleksandrovych Zelenskyy born on 25 January 1978, is a Ukrainian politician, former comedian, and actor, who is serving as the 6th and current president of Ukraine since 2019. He was born into a Ukrainian-Jewish family and grew up speaking Russian as his mother tongue in Kryvyi Rih, a major city of Dnipropetrovsk Oblast in central Ukraine.

Prior to his acting career, he received a law degree from the Kyiv National Economic University. He then pursued a career in comedy and founded Kvartal 95, a production company that produces films, cartoons, and television shows, including his television series, Servants of the People, in which Volodymyr Zelenskyy played the role of the Ukrainian president.

It was broadcast from 2015 to 2019 and gained popularity. A political party with the same name as the TV show was founded in March 2018 by his Kvartal 95 employees. Zelenskyy announced his candidacy for the 2019 presidential election with then-President Petro Poroshenko’s New Year’s speech on TV channel 1+1 on the evening of December 31, 2018.

Volodymyr Zelenskyy Personal Life

Before entering elementary school, Volodymyr Zelensky lived for four years in the Mongolian city of Erdenet, where his father worked. He grew up speaking Russian. At the age of 16, he passed a test for English as a foreign language and received an educational scholarship to study in Israel, but his father did not allow him to go there.

After that, he got a law degree from the Krivi Rie University of Economics (then a department of Kyiv National University of Economics, now part of Krivi Rie National University), but never worked in the legal field.

Volodymyr Zelenskyy then pursued a career in comedy and founded Kvartal 95, a production company that produces films, cartoons, and television shows, including his television series, Servants of the People, in which Volodymyr Zelenskyy played the role of the Ukrainian president.

At the age of 17, he joined a local team and entered the KVN comedy competition team. He was soon invited to join the unified Ukrainian team “Zaporizhia-Kryvyi Rih-Transit” and played in the KVN major league, which he eventually won in 1997.

In the same year, he founded and managed the Kvartal 95 team, which later turned into Comedy Team Kvartal 95. From 1998 to 2003, Kvartal 95 played in the major leagues and the Ukrainian Open League, the best of KVN. He and his team members spent a lot of time in Moscow and toured the post-Soviet countries constantly. In 2003 Kvartal 95 started producing TV programs for the Ukrainian TV channel 1+1 and in 2005 the team moved to the Ukrainian TV channel Inter.

Volodymyr Zelenskyy worked mainly in Russian-language productions. His first role in Ukrainian was in his romantic comedy I, You, He, She was released on Ukrainian screens in December 2018. The first version of the script was written in Ukrainian but was translated into Russian for Lithuanian actress Agnė Grudytė. The film was later dubbed into Ukrainian.
