Alex Morgan career

The Western New York Flash selected Morgan with the first overall pick in the 2011 WPS Draft, just after she had already graduated early from the University of California, Berkeley, where she had played for the California Golden Bears.

She made her professional debut there, aiding the group in capturing the league title. Morgan, who was 22 at the time, was the national team’s youngest player when it finished second in the 2011 FIFA Women’s World Cup.

She scored the game-winning goal against Canada in the semi-final at the 2012 London Olympics in the 123rd minute.

She joined Mia Hamm as the only American woman to score 20 goals and contribute 20 assists in the same calendar year as she concluded 2012 with 28 goals and 21 assists. She was also the sixth and youngest American player to reach 20 goals in a single season.

She later received the U.S. Soccer Female Athlete of the Year award and advanced to the FIFA World Player of the Year finals. Additionally, Morgan contributed to the United States’ victories at the FIFA Women’s World Cups in 2015 and 2019. She was a member of both games’ Dream Team and took home the Silver Boot in 2019.

Morgan joined Portland Thorns FC in 2013, the NWSL’s debut season, and contributed to the team’s league-winning season.

Morgan played for the Thorns through the 2015 season before being traded to the Orlando Pride, a brand-new team. Morgan joined the French team Lyon in 2017, where she won the UEFA Women’s Champions League and the continental European triple.

Off the pitch, Morgan collaborated with Simon & Schuster to create the middle-grade book series The Kicks, which centers on four soccer players. Saving the Team, the first book in the series, released in May 2013 at position seven on The New York Times Best Seller list.

Additionally, Alex & Me, a movie in which Morgan makes her acting debut and portrays a fictionalized version of herself, was released in June 2018.

Due in large part to her multiple sponsorship deals, Morgan was named by Time as the highest-paid American women’s soccer player in 2015.
